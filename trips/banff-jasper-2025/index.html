What are the Paint Pots?

Cold iron-rich mineral springs that stain the surrounding earth vivid orange, red, and ochre. For centuries Indigenous peoples collected this ochre pigment here. Cross the arched bridge over the Vermilion River and follow the flat path to the mineral pools.

♿ Completely flat — ideal for knees

What is Moraine Lake?

A glacially-fed lake in the Valley of the Ten Peaks — formerly on the Canadian $20 bill. The water is an almost unbelievable electric turquoise. In June the glacial melt is at its peak — the colour is at its most intense. The single most visually stunning stop of the entire trip.

Walk the flat lakeshore trail at your pace. The Rockpile viewpoint is a 5-min boulder scramble — skip for knees, the lakeshore view is equally stunning. Canoe rental available (~$30 CAD/hr).

What is Lake Louise?

Perhaps Canada's most famous lake — glacier-fed, dominated by the massive Victoria Glacier, with the Fairmont Chateau Lake Louise (built 1890) at its shore. The colour is a distinct teal-turquoise. June means the glacier is still thick with snow and the colour is at its most intense.

Walk the paved flat lakeshore as far as comfortable. Stop at the Fairmont terrace for a chai or coffee with the glacier view.

♿ Paved flat lakeshore — fully accessible☕ Fairmont terrace — a chai here is a trip highlight

What is the Bow Valley Parkway?

The original Hwy 1 between Lake Louise and Banff — 48 km of quieter scenic road. Far more wildlife sightings than the Trans-Canada: black bears, elk, wolves, bighorn sheep are regularly spotted in June. What is Johnston Canyon?

A narrow limestone canyon with a bolted-in metal catwalk that clings to the canyon walls. The walls close in dramatically as you walk upstream. The Lower Falls thunder through a narrow slot in rock — at their most powerful in June. Sit right on the Bow Valley Parkway, perfect on the way home.

Takakkaw Falls — Canada's 2nd Highest Waterfall
~1 hr 45 min
▼

What is Takakkaw Falls?

Canada's second-highest waterfall at 373 metres. Fed by the Daly Glacier, it plunges 254m in a single unbroken drop, generating a roar and mist cloud you feel from 200m away. June is the absolute best time — maximum glacial melt, maximum power. You feel it before you see it.

An 800m paved path from the parking lot leads to the base — fully accessible. Cross the wooden bridge for the best full-face view. In June the mist is heavy — bring rain jackets, you will get wet.

Height
373m — 2nd in Canada
Walk to base
800m flat, paved
♿ Paved path to base — fully accessible🥽 Bring rain jackets — mist at base is very heavy in June

What is Emerald Lake?

The largest lake in Yoho NP — and arguably the most vividly coloured lake in the entire Canadian Rockies. An almost supernatural emerald-turquoise from glacial rock flour. Surrounded by dense forest with the President Range rising behind. Far quieter than Lake Louise.

Walk the flat lakeside path as far as comfortable. The Emerald Lake Lodge deck is perfect for a cold drink with the lake view. Canoe rental at the dock (~$30/hr).

♿ Flat lakeside path — fully accessible🛥 Canoe rental — colour from water level is extraordinaryAfternoon light 1–4 PM is ideal for photos here

What is Lake Minnewanka?

Banff's largest lake at 24 km long. Below the surface lies a submerged ghost town — the resort village of Minnewanka Landing, flooded in 1941. Drive the scenic loop, stop at the shore pull-offs, then continue to Two Jack Lake and the Hoodoos viewpoint on Tunnel Mountain Drive on the way back.

♿ Drive-up loop — minimal walking needed📷 Red Adirondack chairs at the Hoodoos viewpoint — great family photo

One of the largest lakes in Banff NP at 1,920m elevation. Vivid glacial turquoise at the foot of Crowfoot Glacier (shaped like a crow's foot). What is Peyto Lake?

From above, Peyto Lake is shaped exactly like a howling wolf's head — one of the most iconic views in the Rockies. Electric turquoise fed by Peyto Glacier. At Bow Summit (2,088m) — the highest point on any paved road in Canada. The Mistaya Valley stretches to the horizon below.

What is Mistaya Canyon?

A hidden gem most Parkway drivers miss. A 400m flat trail through forest leads to a dramatic narrow slot canyon carved by the Mistaya River. A bridge over the gorge gives the best view straight down into the churning water. Quick, easy, and completely different in character from everything else.

Why is this stop mandatory?

Fill the tank here — this is the only gas for 154 km north to Jasper and 77 km south to Lake Louise. This remote junction sits at the confluence of three rivers: the North Saskatchewan, the Howse, and the Mistaya. The Crossing Resort has a cafeteria, shop, and restrooms. Stretch, grab snacks, use facilities.
