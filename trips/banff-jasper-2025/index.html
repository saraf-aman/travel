Bow Lake — First Major Lake
~40 min
▼

What is Bow Lake?

One of the largest lakes in Banff NP at 1,920m elevation. Vivid glacial turquoise at the foot of Crowfoot Glacier (shaped like a crow's foot). The historic Num-Ti-Jah Lodge sits on the north shore — built in 1920 by legendary guide Jimmy Simpson. On calm afternoons the mountain reflections are mirror-perfect.

Walk to shore
Flat 5-min trail
Elevation
1,920 m (6,300 ft)
Parking
Large lot off Hwy 93
Facilities
Pit toilets at parking
♿ Flat 5-min walk to shore — fully accessible🌅 Afternoon light turns the glacier warm amber

Peyto Lake — Wolf's Head Lake Iconic
~40 min
▼

What is Peyto Lake?

From above, Peyto Lake is shaped exactly like a howling wolf's head — one of the most iconic views in the Rockies. Electric turquoise fed by Peyto Glacier. At Bow Summit (2,088m) — the highest point on any paved road in Canada. The Mistaya Valley stretches to the horizon below.

Elevation
2,088 m — highest point on Parkway
Walk to viewpoint
400m paved trail, wheelchair accessible
Best time
Afternoon light — 1-4 PM
♿ Fully accessible paved viewpoint platform📷 Most photographed lake in Canadian Rockies
🏛
2:10 PM
Mistaya Canyon — Hidden Gem
~30 min
▼

What is Mistaya Canyon?

A hidden gem most Parkway drivers miss. A 400m flat trail through forest leads to a dramatic narrow slot canyon carved by the Mistaya River. A bridge over the gorge gives the best view straight down into the churning water. Quick, easy, and completely different in character from everything else.

Distance
400m each way, flat
Difficulty
Easy forest walk
♿ Flat 400m trail — knee-friendly🏔 Most drivers skip this — far fewer crowds
⛽
2:50 PM
Saskatchewan River Crossing Gas + Snacks
~30 min
▼

Why is this stop mandatory?

Fill the tank here — this is the only gas for 154 km north to Jasper and 77 km south to Lake Louise. This remote junction sits at the confluence of three rivers: the North Saskatchewan, the Howse, and the Mistaya. The Crossing Resort has a cafeteria, shop, and restrooms. Stretch, grab snacks, use facilities.

⛽ Fill gas here — absolutely do not skip this🍽 Last food/bathrooms for 2+ hours
🏔
3:30 PM
The Weeping Wall — Waterfall Cliff
~10 min photo stop
▼

A massive 100m cliff face where dozens of waterfalls stream down. In June snowmelt is at its peak — the wall literally weeps with water. Quick roadside pull-off — 100m from the car. One of the Parkway's most dramatic features.

♿ Roadside viewpoint — 100m walk📷 June is peak flow — spectacular mist

Why stay in Hinton?

Hinton is 80 km (50 min) east of Jasper townsite. Hotels are 50-70% cheaper than Jasper and you have a stable base for 3 nights. All Jasper highlights are within 1 hour drive. Miette Hot Springs is actually closer to Hinton (30 min) than Jasper (1 hr).
