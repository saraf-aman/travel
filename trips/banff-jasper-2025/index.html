What is Marble Canyon?

A stunning 600-metre slot canyon carved 40 metres deep by Tokumm Creek over 10,000 years. The water is a milky glacial turquoise — rock flour from the glaciers scatters blue light. You cross 7 wooden bridges spanning the gorge, each revealing a completely new perspective, including two natural stone bridges formed by the river itself. At the far end, a hidden waterfall. Far less crowded than Johnston Canyon — raw geological drama at its finest.

Walk the 1.4 km loop at a relaxed pace. Cross every bridge — views are different from each one. Return views are actually better. At the far end, look for the Parks Canada red Adirondack chairs — iconic photo spot with mountain backdrop.

~50 min

What are the Paint Pots?

Cold iron-rich mineral springs that bubble up and stain the surrounding earth vivid shades of orange, red, and ochre. When iron-rich water hits air it oxidises, creating the vivid colouring. For centuries the Ktunaxa and Blackfoot peoples collected this ochre pigment here for body and hide paint — one of the most culturally significant spots in the national parks.

Cross the beautiful arched bridge over the Vermilion River — pause midway, mountain valley view stretches in both directions. Follow the flat path through the ochre beds to the mineral pools. The pools are an eerie green-orange-red swirl.

What are the Vermilion Lakes?

Three connected shallow wetland lakes just west of Banff townsite along a 4.3 km paved road. The lakes perfectly mirror the iconic pyramid of Mount Rundle on calm afternoons. Elk, beavers, great blue herons, ospreys, and ducks are all commonly spotted here. The afternoon light hits the surrounding peaks in warm golden tones.

What is Bow Falls?

The Bow River cascades over a natural rock ledge where it meets the Spray River. The backdrop is the Fairmont Banff Springs Hotel — a Scottish baronial castle built in 1888. The contrast of rushing turquoise water and the grand stone hotel is quintessential Banff. This spot appeared in the 1953 Marilyn Monroe film "River of No Return."

What is the Banff Gondola?

A fully enclosed 8-minute gondola ride to the summit of Sulphur Mountain (2,281m / 7,486 ft). At the top, a 360° panorama across six mountain ranges. The summit boardwalk is paved and flat. Late afternoon in June is one of the best times to ride — golden light turns the limestone peaks warm amber and the valleys fill with long dramatic shadows.

Ride up, walk the paved summit boardwalk, have a hot chai at the Summit Restaurant, take a family photo against the panorama, ride down as the light turns golden. Gondola runs until 9 PM in June — no rush.

What is Moraine Lake?

A glacially-fed lake in the Valley of the Ten Peaks — one of the most photographed places on earth, formerly on the Canadian $20 bill. The water is an almost unbelievable electric turquoise, fed by rock flour from glacial meltwater that scatters blue light. Ten jagged peaks rise steeply around the valley. Accessible only by Parks Canada shuttle in June. The colour peaks in June when glacial melt is highest.

The lakeshore trail is completely flat and paved. The famous Rockpile viewpoint (the $20 bill shot) is a 5-min boulder scramble — skip for knee safety, the lakeshore view is equally stunning.

What is Lake Louise?

Perhaps Canada's most famous lake — glacier-fed, ringed by forested slopes, dominated by the massive Victoria Glacier at its far end. The water colour is an intense teal-turquoise. The Fairmont Chateau Lake Louise (built 1890) sits at the lakeshore. The colour peaks in June and July when glacial meltwater inflow is highest.

What is Takakkaw Falls?

Canada's second-highest waterfall at 373 metres — fed by the Daly Glacier of the Waputik Icefield. "Takakkaw" means "wonderful" in Cree, and the name is earned. The water plunges 254m in one single drop, creating constant mist and a thunder you feel before you see it. June is peak month — maximum water volume from glacial melt. The drive up Yoho Valley Road (15 km of switchbacks through gorges) is spectacular in itself.

An 800m paved path from the parking lot leads to the base — fully accessible. Cross the wooden bridge for the best views. The mist at the base is heavy in June — you may get wet, which is part of the experience.

What are the Spiral Tunnels?

One of Canada's great engineering achievements — two figure-8 tunnels bored through the mountains by the Canadian Pacific Railway in 1909 to tame the impossibly steep Big Hill. A 1,000m upper tunnel through Mount Ogden and an 891m lower tunnel through Cathedral Mountain. When a long freight train passes you can watch the back of the train emerge from the tunnel before the front has even entered — genuinely surreal.

♿ Roadside pull-off — no walking needed🚂 Freight trains run regularly — wait 10-15 min if none on arrival

What is the Bow Valley Parkway?

The original Hwy 1 between Lake Louise and Banff — 48 km of winding scenic road now bypassed by the faster Trans-Canada. The slower speed means dramatically more wildlife sightings: bears, elk, wolves, bighorn sheep, and mountain goats are regularly spotted roadside. Castle Mountain — a dramatically flat-topped cliff face — dominates the view for much of the drive. The best road in the Banff area for wildlife spotting.

What is Johnston Canyon?

A narrow limestone canyon carved by Johnston Creek, with a bolted-in metal catwalk (with handrails) that clings to the canyon walls. The walls close in dramatically as you walk upstream. The Lower Falls thunder through a narrow slot in the rock. Go after 3 PM to significantly reduce crowds. The Upper Falls involve a steep climb with stairs — stick to just the Lower Falls.

What is Lake Minnewanka?

The largest lake in Banff National Park at 24 km long — "Minnewanka" means "Lake of the Water Spirit" in Nakoda. Below the surface lies a submerged ghost town — the resort village of Minnewanka Landing, flooded in 1941 when the lake was dammed for hydroelectric power. A 90-minute boat cruise circles the lake with commentary on the history, wildlife, and geology.

What are the Banff Upper Hot Springs?

Canada's highest-altitude hot spring pool at 1,585m — naturally heated mineral water from deep within Sulphur Mountain, cooled to 37–40°C (99–104°F) for soaking. This is the historic pool where Banff's entire national park story began — the springs were discovered by railway workers in 1883, sparking Canada's first national park in 1885. Excellent for tired muscles and sore knees.

What is the Columbia Icefield?

The largest icefield in the Rocky Mountains — 325 km² of ancient ice up to 365m thick, feeding eight major glaciers. The ice here fell as snow up to 10,000 years ago. The Ice Explorer is a giant specially-built glacier bus that drives you directly onto the ice surface — you step out and walk on the glacier. The Glacier Skywalk is a glass-floored walkway cantilevered 280m above the Sunwapta Valley.

What is Maligne Canyon?

The deepest walkable canyon in the Canadian Rockies — at its deepest point the walls drop 50 metres straight down to Maligne River. Carved through limestone by thousands of years of erosion. The river periodically vanishes underground through karst sinkholes. In June it's running with snowmelt, thunderously dramatic. Go early (before 9 AM) to beat the crowds.

Walk only to Bridges 1 and 2 (800m flat) — shows the deepest and most dramatic canyon section.

♿ First section is flat and accessible⌛ Go before 9 AM — gets very crowded by mid-morning

What is Miette Hot Springs?

The hottest natural mineral springs in the entire Canadian Rockies — source water emerges from the mountain at 54°C (129°F) and is cooled to 40°C for the main soaking pool. The drive to get there is an experience in itself — 61 km east of Jasper through the wild Fiddle Valley. Two hot pools (40°C and 37°C) and two cold plunge pools surrounded by extraordinary mountain scenery.

What is Drumheller?

After 6 days of green mountains, glaciers, and turquoise lakes — Drumheller is the ultimate contrast. The Canadian Badlands are a Mars-like landscape of eroded river canyons, striped walls of red-orange-tan-grey sedimentary rock, and thousands of hoodoos — tall mushroom-shaped pillars of harder rock left standing as softer rock eroded away over 70 million years. This landscape contains some of the richest dinosaur fossil deposits ever found on earth. Late afternoon light turns the canyon walls a deep orange-gold.

Drive the Drumheller Valley Scenic Route. Stop at Horseshoe Canyon viewpoint (drive-up panorama). Walk the Hoodoos Trail (flat 0.5 km paved among the pillars). The World's Largest Dinosaur is a kitschy 26m climbable T-Rex — fun family photo stop.

Practical information
🎫
Parks Canada Pass
~CAD $145/family. Covers Banff, Jasper, Kootenay, Yoho. Buy at reservation.pc.gc.ca or first park gate.
💰
Currency & Payments
Canadian Dollars (CAD). Cards accepted everywhere. 1 USD ≈ 1.35 CAD roughly. Keep some cash for tips.
📵
Cell Service
Zero signal in Kootenay NP and large sections of the Icefields Parkway. Download offline Google Maps for the entire route before leaving Calgary.
⛽
Gas Stations
Fill up at: Calgary, Canmore or Banff, Lake Louise, Saskatchewan Crossing (only mid-Parkway stop!), Jasper. Never assume there's gas ahead.
🌡
June Weather
Banff: 5–20°C. Glacier surface: 0–5°C. Gondola summit: 5–12°C. Drumheller: up to 25°C+. Always bring layers. Rain any day possible.
🐻
Wildlife Safety
Never approach wildlife. Stay 100m from bears/wolves, 30m from all other animals. Drive slowly on Hwy 93 and Bow Valley Parkway in June.
♿
Accessibility Notes
All major stops have accessible parking. Gondola, Skywalk, boat cruises all fully accessible. Ice Explorer requires manual wheelchair (not electric — call ahead).
📺
What to Pack
Layers (fleece + rain jacket essential), swimsuits for hot springs x2, good walking shoes, sunscreen, sunglasses, reusable water bottles, bear spray.
🏥
Medical
Banff Mineral Springs Hospital: 303 Lynx St. Jasper Healthcare Centre: 518 Robson St. Pharmacy in both townsite areas.
🛣
Road Conditions
Check 511.alberta.ca before driving. Yoho Valley Road (Takakkaw Falls) opens mid-June — verify at parks.canada.ca/yoho before Day 3.
📱
Useful Apps
Parks Canada app (trail conditions, alerts), Google Maps offline, iOverlander (free parking spots), Weather Network (mountain forecasts).
🐻
Food in Car Warning
Never leave food visible in car — bear break-ins happen in parking lots. Store all food in the trunk. Applies even to sealed packaging.
