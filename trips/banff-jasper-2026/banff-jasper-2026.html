What are the Paint Pots?

Cold iron-rich mineral springs that stain the surrounding earth vivid orange, red, and ochre. For centuries Indigenous peoples collected this ochre pigment here. Cross the arched bridge over the Vermilion River and follow the flat path to the mineral pools.

What is Moraine Lake?

A glacially-fed lake in the Valley of the Ten Peaks — formerly on the Canadian $20 bill. The water is an almost unbelievable electric turquoise. In June the glacial melt is at its peak — the colour is at its most intense. The single most visually stunning stop of the entire trip.

What is Lake Louise?

Perhaps Canada's most famous lake — glacier-fed, dominated by the massive Victoria Glacier, with the Fairmont Chateau Lake Louise (built 1890) at its shore. The colour is a distinct teal-turquoise. June means the glacier is still thick with snow and the colour is at its most intense.

What is the Ice Explorer?

A massive purpose-built snow coach drives you up onto the Athabasca Glacier — part of the Columbia Icefield, the largest icefield in the Rocky Mountains. You spend 30 minutes standing on 10,000-year-old ice at 2,000m elevation. The ice is moving — the glacier advances about 125m per year. The single most unique experience on the entire trip.

Stargazing — Medicine Lake Optional — clear skies only
~2.5 hrs round trip

Why Medicine Lake?

Jasper National Park is the world's largest accessible Dark Sky Preserve — 11,000 sq km of protected darkness. Medicine Lake sits on Maligne Lake Road, 25 km from Jasper town, completely isolated with no artificial light anywhere nearby. Open water gives star reflections. On a clear night the Milky Way arc is visible with the naked eye. You'll very likely be the only people there.

Drive from Hinton
~50 min each way
Darkness falls
~11 PM (near solstice)
Best viewing
11 PM – 1 AM
Temperature
5–8°C at night — dress warm

Kill the headlights when you arrive. Wait 10 minutes for your eyes to fully adjust. Lie on the car hood and look up — this is one of the darkest accessible skies in North America.

🦉 Bring: blankets, warm layers, red-light headlamp (preserves night vision), bear spray🐻 Bear country at night — stay near the car, make noise, don't wander📱 Check clear sky forecast at cleardarksky.com before leaving🌙 Skip if cloudy — no point driving 50 min for clouds

Maligne Canyon — Deepest Accessible Canyon in the Rockies
~1.5 hrs

What is Maligne Canyon?

The deepest accessible canyon in the Canadian Rockies — walls drop 50 metres, carved by the Maligne River through limestone over thousands of years. Metal catwalks with solid handrails cling to the canyon walls. Six bridges cross the gorge, each revealing a different perspective. Unlike any canyon you've seen this trip.

For accessibility: the 1st and 2nd bridges (20 min in) give dramatic views without difficult terrain. The 3rd bridge is the sweet spot — best overhanging view into the gorge, still manageable. Stop wherever is comfortable and turn back at your own pace.

What is Maligne Lake?

The largest natural lake in the Canadian Rockies at 22 km long. Glacier-fed, surrounded by the Maligne Range. The colour is a brilliant turquoise — distinct from Moraine and Louise. In June the surrounding peaks still carry heavy snow, making the reflections extraordinary.

Walk the flat lakeshore at your own pace before boarding. Grab a coffee at the Maligne Lake Lodge — the deck has a full lake view.

♿ Flat lakeshore — fully accessible☕ Coffee at the lodge deck before the cruise is a good move

What is the Spirit Island Cruise?

A boat takes you 14 km deep into Maligne Lake to Spirit Island — a tiny forested island framed by the Maligne Range, formerly featured on the Canadian $20 bill. Only reachable by boat. The colour of the water at this end of the lake is extraordinary. Glaciers visible from the boat throughout the journey. One of the most iconic views in the Canadian Rockies.

What is Pyramid Lake?

A glacial lake 7 km north of Jasper town at the foot of Pyramid Mountain. A small wooden bridge leads out to Pyramid Island in the middle of the lake — benches, mountain reflection, total quiet. One of the best-known stargazing spots in the preserve. A gentle, peaceful final stop before heading home tomorrow.

♿ Flat 5-min walk to the island bridge — fully accessible📷 Pyramid Mountain reflection in the lake — great final family photo

What is Sunwapta Falls?

A two-tiered waterfall just off the Parkway that most drivers walk past on the Day 4 northbound drive. The Sunwapta River drops into a narrow canyon — quieter than Athabasca Falls but with a beautiful horseshoe shape. 5-minute flat walk from the parking area. Genuinely new — you haven't seen this one yet.
